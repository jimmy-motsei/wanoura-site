Why a WhatsApp Line? — Benefits & Cons

Benefits

  • Low friction & familiar for South African customers (near-universal adoption).
  • Rich media: photos, voice notes, location, documents.
  • Async convenience: customers reply on their time; good for busy schedules.
  • Instant notifications; no separate app or portal required.
  • Cost-effective entry point for small teams; quick to launch.
  • Works well for simple Q&A, quick order checks, and updates.

Cons

  • High manual workload; hard to scale beyond a few agents.
  • Inconsistent data capture; weak CRM history & reporting.
  • Limited automation; complex flows (booking, triage) are clunky.
  • Compliance & privacy risk if conversations live on personal devices.
  • Agent collision & context loss across multiple chats.
  • Limited analytics/attribution; difficult to measure conversion.

Why an AI-Powered Chatbot? — Benefits & Cons

Benefits

  • 24/7 responses with consistent, premium brand tone.
  • Scales to peak demand without extra staff.
  • Built-in flows: booking/reschedule/cancel, reminders, waitlist.
  • HubSpot integration: lead capture, preferences, full history.
  • Personalized recommendations (RAG/AI Search + product rules).
  • Multilingual support (English, Afrikaans, local languages).
  • Analytics for CSAT, containment, conversion, AOV, time saved.

Cons

  • Initial setup & integration effort (WP, Azure, HubSpot, scheduler).
  • Ongoing maintenance: content, FAQs, models, guardrails.
  • Edge cases still need smooth human handoff and SLAs.
  • Requires high-quality knowledge sources to avoid bad answers.
  • Change management: staff training & process alignment.
  • Cloud dependency & data governance considerations.
